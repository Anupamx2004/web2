Appropriate Cropping Pattern

Jammu & Kashmir

Kashmir is known for paddy (rice), while Jammu focuses on wheat, maize, pulses, oilseeds, and vegetables. Ladakh primarily cultivates barley.

Rajasthan

Rajasthan follows Kharif (summer) and Rabi (winter) cropping with key crops like pearl millet (bajra), wheat, pulses, mustard, guar, and maize.

Bihar

Bihar has a diverse cropping pattern including rice, wheat, maize, pulses, vegetables, fruits, sugarcane, and jute.

Assam

Assam mainly cultivates rice, along with rapeseed, mustard, tea, pulses, jute, sugarcane, potatoes, and horticultural crops.

Cauvery Delta Zone (Tamil Nadu)

Rice is the primary crop in the Cauvery Delta Zone. The cropping system includes Rice-Rice-Blackgram or Rice-Rice-Sesame.
